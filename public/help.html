how to play


(1/4) join a lobby

enter a nickname and choose one of four possible lobbies to play in: olympia, corinth (60-), athens (45-30), or sparta (15-15).

lobbies restrict the amount of time you have to pick heroes and to play your turn.

heroes are randomly assigned to teams in olympia, and turns are untimed. assigned teams will have at least one fighter class and at least one healer class.

corinth gives 60 seconds to pick heroes, and turns are untimed.

athens gives 45 seconds to pick heroes and 30 seconds per turn.

sparta gives 15 seconds to pick heroes and 15 seconds per turn.

(2/4) pick your heroes

pick up to five hero cards by clicking in the index to bring a card into your deck.

you can view hero details by hovering over any card with your mouse, and you can click on any card in your deck to return it to the index.

there are seven available hero classes: melee and ranged fighters, protectors and healers, supporters and casters, and special heroes.

classes can be previewed at any time.

  • melee and ranged fighters tend to have higher attack (ATK) and primarily do damage through autoattacks and abilities.

  • protectors focus on absorbing damage or increasing the defense (DEF) of allies, while the main purpose of healers is to restore allies' health points (HP).

  • supporters boost allies' stats and bestow various blessings, while casters impair enemies and worsen enemy stats.

  • special heroes use unique abilities to affect the outcome of the game.

if you haven't picked any heroes when the allotted time is up, you and your opponent will be returned to the lobby.

(3/4) move your hero

each player has two turns per round.

on your turn, click one of your tokens on the map to move it, and click again to place it.

note that clicking the same tile again will keep the token in the same position for the turn. to unselect a tile, press c instead.

the maximum distance that you can move the token depends on the hero's movement (MVT) stat.

at any point, you can press z and x to decrease/increase the preview radius, which appears when you hover your cursor over a game tile.

the preview radius can be useful as a tool for measuring tile distances on the grid.

(4/4) autoattack, use your ability, or use your ultimate

the hero's autoattack will attack all opponents within their reach (RC).

each hero also has a unique ability consuming 200 mana points (MP), and an ultimate consuming 1000 MP.

all heroes gain a certain amount of MP after each turn, and generally can accumulate up to 1000 MP.

in addition to an ability and an ultimate, each hero has a passive skill that is automatically applied each turn.

ability, ultimate, and passive descriptions can be previewed at any time.

damaging opponents

the base amount of damage done is usually calculated with the hero's ATK divided by their opponent's DEF.

there are six status effects that modify the base amount of damage:

  1. charmed: the hero's DEF is reduced by 50%.
  2. distracted: the hero is prevented from gaining MP, and DEF is reduced by 25%.
  3. poisoned: the hero will obtain 800/DEF damage each turn.
  4. stunned: the hero's ATK and MVT are reduced to zero, and RC is reduced to one.
  5. terrified: the hero's ATK is reduced by 50%.
  6. obscured: the hero's location is not visible to the opponent.

as an example, a terrified hero with ATK=2000 would deal (2000/10)/2=100 HP of damage to a hero with DEF=10.

status effects typically persist for 4 turns; repeated applications will stack by duration. status effects can be removed by certain heroes.

true damage is applied directly and is not affected by hero stats or statuses.

heroes are defeated when their HP reaches zero. a hero's passive becomes inactive once they are defeated.

mythomagic is won when all enemy heroes have been defeated, or when your opponent concedes.

creating an account

if you have an invitation code, you can create an account.

your account username reserves a nickname for your usage and assigns you an invitation code that can be used up to five times.

please do not create multiple accounts to gain more invitation codes: your account (and all resulting accounts) will eventually be removed.

accounts track your wins and losses across games and calculate your player score, which is used to determine your class and ranking.

your player score is initialized to be 10.00.

winning a game against a logged-in player grants you 1 point plus 10% of your opponent's score; surrendering a game after pick phase completes counts as a win towards your opponent.

if a player is logged in, their name in the lobby will have an asterisk (*) with a color denoting their current class.

classes are assigned based on your score: iron, bronze (>15.00), silver (>20.00), gold (>30.00), grandmaster (>40.00).
